## Preface

I first encountered Origen while exploring one of Christianity's oldest and most difficult questions: the nature of hell.

Like many Christians, I had inherited certain assumptions about judgment, salvation, and the destiny of the human soul. Yet as I began reading more deeply, I discovered that the history of Christian thought was far richer and more diverse than I had imagined. Early Christian believers wrestled with many of the same questions that people continue to ask today. What is the purpose of God's judgment? Is punishment an end in itself, or can it serve a restorative purpose? What does it mean for God to be both just and loving? What is the final destiny of creation?

As I followed those questions backward through history, one name appeared again and again: Origen of Alexandria.

What first drew my attention was his belief in the ultimate restoration of all things. Yet what kept me reading was something deeper. I found a thinker of remarkable imagination and spiritual depth. Again and again, Origen refused to settle for easy answers. He approached scripture not merely as a collection of historical events or religious doctrines, but as a living treasury of wisdom inviting readers into an ever-deepening understanding of God.

The farther I read, the more I realized that many of the questions that occupied Origen nearly eighteen centuries ago are still our questions. What does it mean to grow spiritually? Why does suffering exist? What is the purpose of creation? How should we understand freedom, virtue, and transformation? What does it mean to become united with God?

These are not merely theological questions. They are human questions.

### Who Was Origen?

Origen was born around AD 185 in Alexandria, Egypt, one of the intellectual centers of the ancient world. He lived during a formative period in Christian history, before many doctrines had been formally defined and before the boundaries of Christian theology had fully taken shape.

He was a teacher, biblical scholar, theologian, and spiritual guide whose influence on Christianity is difficult to overstate. His writings helped shape Christian approaches to scripture, theology, prayer, and spiritual formation for centuries to come. Many of the methods of biblical interpretation later embraced throughout the Christian tradition owe a significant debt to his work.

Origen believed that scripture contained layers of meaning. Beneath the surface of its stories and commands lay deeper spiritual truths waiting to be discovered. He encouraged readers not merely to read the text, but to wrestle with it, meditate upon it, and allow it to transform them.

He was also unafraid to ask difficult questions. Throughout his writings he explored the nature of God, free will, the human soul, creation, judgment, resurrection, and the destiny of the universe itself.

Some of Origen's ideas became controversial in later centuries, and not all of his conclusions were embraced by the wider Church. Yet even those who disagreed with him often found themselves engaging with questions he was among the first to articulate. His influence can be seen across both Eastern and Western Christianity, and his legacy continues to inspire discussion and debate to this day.

Few figures have cast a longer shadow across Christian thought.

### Why This Book Exists

*On First Principles* is one of the earliest systematic works of Christian theology ever written. It is also one of the most challenging.

The original text is dense, philosophical, and often difficult for modern readers to follow. It was written for an audience living in a very different world, familiar with modes of argument and patterns of thought that can feel foreign to contemporary readers.

Yet hidden within its pages is a profound spiritual vision.

This book was created to make that vision more accessible.

My goal was not to write a scholarly commentary, nor to modernize Origen into someone he never was. Instead, I sought to preserve the questions he explored, the reasoning he followed, and the conclusions he reached while expressing them in language that modern readers can more easily understand.

Each reading focuses on a central idea from the text. Technical language has been simplified. Long arguments have been condensed. Historical details have often been left aside in order to highlight the deeper spiritual insights that lie at the heart of Origen's thought.

The result is not a replacement for *On First Principles*. Readers interested in studying Origen's work in its original form should absolutely do so. Rather, this book is intended to serve as a companion—a bridge between an ancient text and modern readers.

I have attempted to present Origen not as a distant historical figure, but as a thoughtful guide inviting us into conversation about some of life's deepest questions.

### An Invitation

You may not agree with every conclusion Origen reaches.

I certainly do not.

Yet I have found him to be a wise companion in the search for God.

One of the gifts Origen offers is a refusal to remain at the surface of things. Again and again, he invites us to look deeper. Beneath scripture's stories lies spiritual meaning. Beneath ordinary experience lies the possibility of transformation. Beneath our limited understanding lies a reality far greater than we can fully comprehend.

He reminds us that faith is not merely a collection of answers. It is a journey into wisdom.

My hope is that these daily readings serve as an accessible companion to one of Christianity's most remarkable thinkers. More importantly, I hope they encourage you to ponder more deeply the character and nature of God, the purpose of spiritual growth, and the mystery of our place within creation.

If, after reading these pages, you find yourself more attentive to truth, more open to wonder, more charitable toward others, and more deeply rooted in faith, then this book will have accomplished its purpose.

May these readings encourage you to think deeply, seek earnestly, and continue the lifelong journey toward the One who is the source of all wisdom, truth, and love.
