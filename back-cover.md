*On First Principles* is one of the most influential works in the history of Christian thought—and one of the most difficult.

Written in the third century by Origen of Alexandria, this remarkable work explores some of the deepest questions of faith: Who is God? Why was the world created? What is the nature of the soul? How should we read Scripture? What is the purpose of spiritual growth? And what is the ultimate destiny of creation?

Yet for many modern readers, Origen's original text can feel dense, philosophical, and inaccessible.

This book offers a different approach.

Through 116 short daily readings, Origen's ideas are translated into clear, contemporary language while preserving the heart of his arguments and the wonder of his vision. Each reading invites you to walk alongside one of Christianity's greatest early thinkers as he wrestles with questions that remain just as relevant today as they were eighteen centuries ago.

Whether you are exploring the Church Fathers for the first time, seeking a deeper understanding of Christian theology, or simply looking for thoughtful daily reflections, this volume serves as a companion for the journey.

You may not agree with every conclusion Origen reaches.

But you will encounter a mind devoted to seeking wisdom, a faith unafraid of difficult questions, and a vision of God that continues to inspire readers nearly two thousand years later.

Spend a few minutes each day with one of the early Church's most fascinating voices—and discover why his questions still matter today.
