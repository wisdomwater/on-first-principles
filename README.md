# Origen's *On First Principles* — Modern Daily Reading Edition

A modern daily reading adaptation of Origen of Alexandria's *On First Principles*.

This project transforms one of Christianity's earliest and most influential theological works into a series of short, accessible daily readings designed for contemporary readers.

Rather than producing a scholarly commentary or a literal paraphrase, the goal is to preserve Origen's central arguments while presenting them in clear modern language that can be read devotionally or reflectively.

---

## About the Project

Origen's *On First Principles* is one of the foundational works of Christian theology. Written in the third century, it explores questions such as:

* Who is God?
* What is the nature of the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit?
* Why was the world created?
* What is the purpose of human life?
* How should Scripture be interpreted?
* What is the destiny of the soul?
* What does spiritual growth look like?

While profoundly influential, the original work can be difficult for modern readers due to its age, style, and philosophical vocabulary.

This project seeks to make Origen's ideas accessible without losing the substance of his reasoning.

---

## Project Goals

Each reading aims to:

* Preserve Origen's central question.
* Follow his line of reasoning.
* Present his conclusion accurately.
* Explain why the idea matters.
* Connect the discussion to ordinary human experience.
* Remain readable in approximately two minutes.

The intention is not to replace the original text but to serve as a bridge between Origen and modern readers.

---

## Adaptation Philosophy

This project follows several guiding principles:

### Preserve the Argument

The primary goal is faithfulness to Origen's reasoning.

Each entry should help readers understand:

1. The question being explored.
2. The argument being made.
3. The conclusion reached.
4. Why the conclusion matters.

### Accessibility Over Technicality

Technical theological language is simplified whenever possible.

Historical debates and scholarly disputes are generally omitted unless essential to understanding the argument.

### Companion, Not Commentary

The readings are written as though a thoughtful guide is walking beside the reader.

The focus is on the ideas themselves rather than on explaining what "Origen believed."

### Wonder Over Polemics

The project seeks to preserve the sense of curiosity, exploration, and spiritual wonder that characterizes much of Origen's work.

---

## Reading Format

Each entry follows the format:

```text
## Day X — Title

> Short quotation from the source text

5–6 paragraphs exploring the idea in modern language.
```

Typical length:

* 450–600 words
* Approximately 2 minutes reading time

---

## Structure

The completed work contains:

* Preface
* 116 Daily Readings
* Epilogue

The readings progress through the major themes of *On First Principles*, including:

* The nature of God
* The Trinity
* Christ and the Logos
* Creation
* Rational beings
* Freedom and moral responsibility
* The soul
* Scripture and interpretation
* Divine providence
* Judgment and restoration
* Resurrection and the age to come

---

## Source Material

Primary source:

**Origen of Alexandria — *On First Principles* (*De Principiis*)**

The adaptation is based on modern English translations of the surviving text.

This project does not attempt to resolve textual, historical, or theological controversies surrounding Origen's work. The focus is on making the ideas accessible to contemporary readers.

---

## Intended Audience

This project may be useful for:

* Readers interested in the Church Fathers
* Students of Christian history
* Spiritual seekers
* Devotional readers
* Small groups and discussion groups
* Readers exploring Christian mysticism and early theology

No prior knowledge of Origen is required.
