## 17 — Even Light Can Fall

> *“How has Lucifer, who arose in the morning, fallen from heaven?”*

Many people assume that goodness and evil belong to different kinds of beings. Some are naturally good, they say, while others are naturally corrupt. Some were created for light and others for darkness.

But if this were true, how could anyone be praised or blamed?

A being created good and incapable of becoming otherwise deserves no credit for its goodness. Likewise, a being created evil deserves no blame for its wickedness. In both cases, character would be determined by nature rather than choice. Virtue would not be an achievement, and evil would not be a failure. Both would simply be conditions imposed from the beginning.

For this reason, we should reject the idea that any rational creature was created either holy or wicked by necessity.

The prophets themselves testify otherwise. Ezekiel speaks of a glorious being who once dwelt among holy things, adorned with beauty and honor, yet in whom iniquity was later found. Isaiah describes Lucifer, the morning star, who fell from heaven because pride arose within him. These passages reveal a profound truth: evil is not an original substance. It is the distortion of a good thing.

Consider carefully what this means.

The one who fell was once called a light-bearer. He was not darkness from the beginning. He possessed splendor. He knew goodness. Yet through pride he turned away from the source of his life. The fall was not imposed upon him. It was chosen.

This should awaken both caution and hope within us.

It is a warning because no created condition is permanently secure by its own power. A soul does not remain in the light merely because it has once known the light. Negligence slowly weakens devotion. Pride quietly separates the heart from wisdom. What begins as a small turning away can, if left unchecked, become a great distance.

Yet there is hope hidden within the same truth.

If evil is not part of anyone’s original nature, then it is not the deepest truth about anyone. Darkness is not a substance that exists on its own. It is what remains when light is abandoned. The soul was not created for corruption. It was created for participation in God. Even when it wanders far, the memory of its true homeland remains.

Therefore, do not become proud when you stand, nor despair when you fall.

The highest angel can descend through self-exaltation. The lowest sinner can begin to rise through humility. Every rational creature lives within this freedom. We are not fixed in place. We are always becoming.

The question is not what we were yesterday, nor even what we have become today. The question is toward what we are turning now.

For every movement toward love is a movement toward our true nature, and every movement toward God is a return to the light from which we first received our being.
