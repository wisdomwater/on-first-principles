## 6 — The Pattern Behind Everything

> *"Wisdom contains within herself both the beginnings and causes and forms of the whole creation."*

Imagine standing before a great cathedral. Your eyes take in stone, glass, arches, and carvings. Yet long before any of those materials were assembled, the cathedral existed in another form. It lived first as an idea. An architect carried within his mind a vision of the whole before a single stone was laid. Every window, every column, every doorway was already present as a pattern waiting to be expressed.

Something similar can be said about creation itself. The world is not a random collection of disconnected things. Beneath the visible universe lies a deeper order, an underlying wisdom from which all things arise. The beauty of a sunset, the laws that govern the stars, the instincts of living creatures, the capacity for love, truth, and reason—all of these point toward a reality that is not chaos but meaning.

When the ancient writers spoke of Christ as Wisdom, they were inviting us to see something profound. Christ is not merely a teacher who appeared at a particular moment in history. He is the eternal wisdom through which all things hold together. Before there were mountains, oceans, or galaxies, there was already the divine intelligence that would give them form. Before there were human minds capable of seeking truth, there was the source from which truth itself would flow.

This helps explain why moments of discovery can feel strangely sacred. Think of a scientist uncovering a hidden law of nature, an artist finding the perfect expression for an idea, or a parent suddenly understanding a child in a new way. In each case, something concealed becomes visible. Something that was always true is finally recognized. We often call these moments insight, but perhaps they are also encounters with wisdom itself.

There is comfort in this perspective. Many of us move through seasons that feel confusing and fragmented. Plans collapse. Relationships change. Circumstances unfold in ways we never expected. It is easy to assume that life is little more than a collection of accidents. Yet wisdom invites us to trust that there may be a deeper coherence than we can presently see. The pattern is often larger than our immediate field of vision. A single thread may seem meaningless until it is viewed within the tapestry.

This does not mean every event is easy to understand. Much remains mysterious. Yet it suggests that reality itself is grounded in intelligence rather than absurdity, in purpose rather than emptiness. The search for wisdom is therefore not an attempt to escape the world. It is a way of seeing the world more clearly. Every act of learning, every movement toward truth, every glimpse of beauty becomes a participation in the deeper pattern from which all things come.

The invitation today is to look beyond appearances. Beneath the countless details of your life, beneath the worries that occupy your attention, beneath even the questions you cannot yet answer, there may be a wisdom quietly holding everything together. You may not see the entire design, but you can learn to trust the One in whom the design resides.
