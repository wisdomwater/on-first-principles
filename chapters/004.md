## 4 — The Horizon Beyond Understanding

> *"We must believe that God is far greater than our thoughts about him."*

Every understanding of God is both true and incomplete.

This is not because God is hiding. It is because the Infinite cannot be contained within the limits of a finite mind. No matter how profound our understanding becomes, there is always more to discover.

Imagine trying to understand the sun by studying a single beam of light entering through a window. The beam is real. It genuinely comes from the sun. Yet it cannot communicate the full brilliance, heat, and power of its source. The beam reveals something true while leaving much unseen.

Our knowledge of God often works in a similar way.

We encounter beauty and sense something greater behind it. We experience love and glimpse a reality larger than ourselves. We discover truth, goodness, and wonder, and through them we catch sight of their source. These experiences are not illusions. They are rays of a deeper light.

Yet there is a temptation to mistake the ray for the sun. We become attached to our concepts, our definitions, and our descriptions. We begin to think that because we can say something true about God, we have somehow exhausted the mystery.

Humility protects us from this mistake. Humility does not deny what we know. It simply recognizes that every insight opens onto a larger horizon. The more we learn, the more we discover how vast reality truly is.

This realization need not produce frustration. It can produce wonder. A mystery that can be completely mastered soon becomes uninteresting. The greatest realities remain endlessly rich. They continue revealing new depths no matter how long we explore them.

Perhaps spiritual growth is not about reaching the end of understanding. Perhaps it is about learning to stand in awe before a reality that forever exceeds our grasp while continually inviting us closer.
