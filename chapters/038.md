## 38 — Learning to See

> *“The Holy Spirit shall come and enlighten them.”*

There are times when two people can read the same words and yet see entirely different things.

One person notices only the surface. Another perceives a deeper meaning hidden within. The text itself has not changed, but something in the reader has.

This as one of the primary works of the Holy Spirit.

When we look at the history of God's people, we notice that spiritual insight often appeared only in scattered individuals. A prophet might glimpse a deeper truth. A wise teacher might perceive a hidden meaning. But for most people, sacred teachings remained largely external. They followed the words without always understanding the reality toward which the words pointed.

Then something changed.

After Christ, spiritual understanding began spreading far beyond a handful of prophets and sages. Ordinary men and women, many with little education and no philosophical training, began recognizing meanings within Scripture that previously seemed hidden. They sensed that some commands pointed beyond themselves. They saw symbols where earlier generations had seen only rituals. They discovered spiritual realities beneath literal forms.

How did this happen?

The answer is simple: the Holy Spirit teaches the soul how to see.

Think of learning to appreciate music. At first you may hear only sounds. Over time you begin noticing melodies, harmonies, and patterns that were always present but previously escaped your attention. Nothing new was added to the music. What changed was your ability to hear.

The Spirit works in a similar way.

This does not mean that everyone receives exactly the same gift. Just as sunlight illuminates different landscapes according to their shape and character, the Spirit reveals different aspects of divine life according to the needs and capacities of each person. One receives wisdom. Another receives understanding. Another receives faith. Another receives courage. The source is the same, but its expression varies.

This is why Origen describes the Spirit as meeting people where they are. The Spirit does not force every soul into the same mold. Rather, the divine presence becomes what each person most needs for growth. To one person the Spirit may appear primarily as comfort. To another as conviction. To another as insight. To another as strength.

Yet beneath these different experiences lies a common purpose.

The Spirit opens the eyes of the heart.

We often imagine spiritual growth as acquiring more information. We think enlightenment means learning new facts. But many of life's deepest truths are not hidden because they are secret. They are hidden because we do not yet know how to perceive them.

The work of the Spirit is to awaken that perception.

A familiar passage suddenly becomes alive. A truth heard many times before begins to shine with new meaning. A connection appears where none was seen before. The world itself begins to look different.

The words remain the same. The reality was always there.

What has changed is that we have begun, however faintly, to see.
