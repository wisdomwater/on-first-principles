## 99 — Why the Truth Is Hidden

> *"The aim was that not everyone who wished should have these mysteries laid before his feet."*

Why doesn't Scripture simply state everything plainly?

It is a question many readers eventually ask. If spiritual truth is so important, why is it wrapped in stories, symbols, parables, prophecies, and mysteries? Why not present everything directly and remove all ambiguity?

Part of the answer lies in the nature of wisdom itself. Information can be handed to someone instantly, but understanding cannot. A teacher may explain a profound truth in a single sentence, yet that truth may take years to fully grasp. Some realities can only be understood through reflection, experience, and inner growth. They must be discovered, not merely delivered.

This is why so much of Scripture speaks through images and narratives. The stories invite us into a process rather than merely providing conclusions. They awaken curiosity. They encourage searching. They reward patience. The hiddenness is not meant to exclude sincere seekers but to draw them deeper into the search itself.

Think of a mountain trail. If someone were carried by helicopter directly to the summit, they would see the view, but they would miss the journey that prepared them to appreciate it. The climb strengthens the body, develops endurance, and gradually expands the horizon. By the time the summit is reached, the traveler has been changed by the ascent. In much the same way, spiritual understanding often comes through the process of seeking rather than through immediate disclosure.

This does not mean that Scripture is intended only for scholars or intellectuals. The simple and obvious teachings remain available to everyone. Anyone can find guidance, comfort, and encouragement in its pages. Yet beneath those accessible truths lie deeper treasures for those who continue digging. The same text that nourishes a beginner can challenge a mature seeker for a lifetime.

There is also a protective wisdom in this arrangement. Profound truths can easily be misunderstood when approached carelessly. A person who seeks only arguments may miss the wisdom entirely. Another who approaches with humility and perseverance may discover insights that transform the whole of life. The hiddenness acts less like a locked door and more like a filter, inviting sincerity while discouraging superficiality.

The purpose, then, is not secrecy for its own sake. The goal is transformation. Scripture does not merely wish to inform the mind; it seeks to shape the soul. By requiring attention, reflection, and patience, it gradually forms the very qualities needed to receive its deepest insights. The mystery is not an obstacle placed in the way. It is part of the path itself.
