## 7 — The Light That Never Leaves

> *"He is the brightness of the eternal light and an image of his goodness."*

Have you ever noticed that we understand many things only because something else reveals them? We do not see objects in a dark room by staring harder. We see them when light falls upon them. The light does not compete with the object for our attention. It simply makes the object visible.

In much the same way, there are realities that cannot be grasped merely by effort. You can spend years asking questions about God, truth, goodness, or the meaning of life, yet still feel as though you are standing in a dimly lit room. Information alone cannot solve the problem. What is needed is illumination—a light by which deeper things become visible.

This is why wisdom is often described as light. Not because wisdom gives us more facts, but because it changes how we see. A person may live in the same house, work the same job, and encounter the same people as before, yet after gaining wisdom, everything appears different. The world itself has not changed. Their sight has.

Think about the difference between memorizing a definition of love and actually experiencing love. One belongs to the mind; the other fills the whole person. Or consider the moment when a difficult lesson suddenly "clicks." The facts may have been sitting in front of you for months, but then something shifts. Understanding arrives like dawn. What was hidden becomes clear.

The same is true of our search for God. We often imagine that God is distant, concealed somewhere beyond the reach of ordinary life. Yet perhaps the greater challenge is not distance but perception. The sun may be shining brilliantly outside, but a person with closed eyes remains in darkness. The problem is not the absence of light. The problem is learning how to receive it.

The ancient image of light flowing from light points toward a profound mystery. Light does not diminish itself by shining. A candle can kindle a thousand other candles without losing any of its own flame. In the same way, truth can be shared without being reduced. Love can be given without being depleted. Wisdom can illuminate countless minds while remaining whole. The source is never exhausted by its generosity.

Perhaps this is why moments of genuine insight feel so alive. They are reminders that reality is not ultimately dark or meaningless. Beneath confusion, fear, and uncertainty, there remains a light that has never stopped shining. Our task is not to manufacture it. Our task is to turn toward it, allow it to illuminate us, and then carry that light into the lives of others.
