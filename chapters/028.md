## 28 — No Endless Repetition

> *"Souls are not driven on some revolving course which brings them into the same cycle again."*

When people imagine eternity, they often picture one of two possibilities. Some envision a final state where everything becomes fixed and unchanging forever. Others imagine an endless cycle in which the same events repeat again and again, like a wheel turning through the same patterns without end.

But neither possibility seems to fit the reality of freedom.

The discussion begins with a question about the destiny of the body. If death is ultimately defeated, and if souls continue to grow in wisdom and union with God, might there come a point when bodies are no longer needed? Some thinkers believed so. They imagined a future in which material existence gradually fades away until only spiritual reality remains. At first glance, this seems reasonable. If the soul becomes increasingly illuminated by divine life, perhaps the body eventually falls away like a cocoon after a butterfly emerges.

Yet there is a difficulty. If rational creatures always retain freedom, then their story cannot be reduced to a static condition. Freedom means movement, choice, growth, and relationship. And wherever there is genuine freedom, there remains the possibility of new developments. If creatures continue to exist as free beings, then creation itself remains dynamic rather than frozen into a permanent state.

This leads to a second question. If there are many ages or worlds, do they simply repeat one another? Will history eventually circle back and begin again exactly as before? The answer given here is an emphatic no. A world identical in every detail to the present one would require every choice to be repeated exactly. Adam and Eve would act the same way. Moses would lead the same people. Judas would make the same betrayal. Every conversation, every decision, every moment of history would recur without variation.

But that would mean freedom is an illusion.

To show how unlikely this is, consider scattering a bushel of grain onto the ground. Even if you repeated the experiment thousands of times, the grains would never land in precisely the same arrangement. The possibilities are simply too numerous. How much less likely would it be for billions of free choices made by countless rational creatures to unfold in exactly the same pattern again? History may rhyme, but it does not duplicate itself.

This insight reveals something profound about the nature of creation. The future is not merely a replay of the past. God's work is not a giant wheel turning endlessly through identical cycles. Rather, creation is an unfolding story shaped by living beings whose choices matter. There may be many ages. There may be worlds beyond worlds. There may be depths of existence we cannot yet imagine. But whatever lies ahead will not be a carbon copy of what has already been.

The hope held out here is that reality remains open. The journey toward God is not trapped in repetition. Every act of growth creates new possibilities. Every step toward wisdom opens further horizons. The ages to come are not empty extensions of the present but opportunities for an ever-deepening participation in truth, goodness, and divine life. Freedom ensures that the story remains alive, and because the story remains alive, the future can hold genuine surprise.
