## 9 — The Power That Was Always There

> *“Through wisdom God is called Almighty.”*

We often think about God in relation to the world. God creates, guides, sustains, and governs. Yet this raises an interesting question. Was God somehow different before there was a creation to govern?

At first the question may seem strange, but follow it carefully. If there were once a time when God had no creation, then there would have been a time when God was not exercising the fullness of his power. Later, when creation came into being, God would have become something he previously was not. He would have gained a new status or entered a higher condition. Yet can the perfect source of all things truly improve? Can the eternal become more complete than it already is?

God does not grow, progress, or acquire missing qualities. Whatever belongs truly to God belongs to him eternally. For this reason, divine wisdom cannot be a later addition to God's life. Nor can divine power. God's wisdom, God's Word, and God's power are not tools that he picks up when needed. They belong to his very being.

This is why Christ is described as the power of God and the wisdom of God. Through wisdom, all things are ordered. Through reason, all things are held together. The universe is not governed through brute force but through intelligibility. A king may rule through fear, compelling obedience by threats and violence. But wisdom governs differently. It persuades, illuminates, orders, and draws things toward their proper place. The deepest power is not coercion but understanding.

Look at the world around you. The planets move according to patterns. Seeds become trees according to hidden principles. Minds discover mathematical truths that seem woven into reality itself. Everywhere we encounter order, proportion, and meaning. For Origen, these are signs that the universe rests upon wisdom rather than chaos. Creation is not held together by arbitrary force but by a rational principle that gives coherence to everything that exists.

This insight also helps explain why Christ is called the "effluence" or outpouring of God's glory. When sunlight fills a room, it does not diminish the sun. It reveals its presence. In the same way, divine wisdom reveals divine power. Through Christ, the hidden order and goodness of God become visible within creation. The glory of God is not merely raw might. It is power expressed through wisdom, harmony, and purpose.

We can draw one final distinction. Created beings may become wise, good, or righteous, but these qualities can increase or diminish. We grow in wisdom and sometimes lose it. We become faithful and sometimes falter. Our virtues are gifts we participate in rather than qualities that belong to us by nature. But divine wisdom is different. It cannot fade, fluctuate, or be lost. What God is, God eternally is.

That conviction lies beneath the entire argument. The source of reality is not a changing being struggling toward greater perfection. The source of reality is already complete. And because divine wisdom is eternal, the light that guides creation is not something temporary or fragile. It is woven into the very foundation of existence itself.
