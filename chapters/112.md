## 112 — The Endless Depth of Wisdom

> *“O the depth of the riches of the wisdom and knowledge of God!”*

One of the great temptations in spiritual life is the belief that, given enough study, enough effort, or enough time, we might eventually understand everything. We often approach truth as though it were a puzzle that can finally be completed. Yet there are some realities that grow larger the closer we come to them. The wisdom of God is one of these.

Think of a traveler standing on the shore of the ocean. From a distance, the sea appears simple enough. But the farther he ventures, the more he discovers. Beneath the surface lie currents, depths, creatures, and mysteries he never imagined. Each discovery reveals not an ending but a doorway into something larger. The pursuit of divine wisdom follows a similar pattern. Every genuine insight opens the way to further questions, deeper understanding, and wider horizons.

This helps explain why even the greatest spiritual minds speak with humility. The apostle Paul, after exploring profound truths about God and creation, does not announce that he has solved every mystery. Instead, he marvels. “O the depth of the riches of the wisdom and knowledge of God!” His response is not mastery but wonder. The closer he comes to the mystery, the more clearly he sees its vastness.

The same principle applies to every created mind. No matter how much we learn, there will always remain more to discover. A child can spend years studying the stars and still not exhaust the heavens. A scientist may devote a lifetime to understanding nature and uncover only a fraction of its secrets. If this is true of the created world, how much more must it be true of the One from whom all creation comes?

For this reason, spiritual growth is not a journey toward possessing all knowledge. It is a journey into ever-deepening participation in truth. The goal is not to stand at the end and say, “I understand everything,” but to become increasingly capable of receiving what can be known. Even the highest spiritual beings, however near they may be to the divine presence, do not comprehend the fullness of God's wisdom. They see farther than we do, but the mystery remains greater still.

Far from discouraging us, this should fill us with hope. If God's wisdom could be exhausted, our growth would eventually come to an end. There would be nothing left to discover, nothing left to become. Instead, reality is richer than that. Every step forward reveals new beauty, new understanding, and new possibilities. The invitation is not to conquer the mystery but to continue pursuing it. With each act of faith, each deeper insight, and each movement toward goodness, we are reaching into an inexhaustible ocean whose depths no creature can fully measure, yet whose waters are always open to those willing to venture farther.
