## 72 — The Unseen Struggle

> *"Our wrestling is not against flesh and blood."*

When people think about the causes of wrongdoing, they usually look in one of two directions. Some point inward. Human weakness, selfishness, fear, pride, and desire explain why we go astray. Others point outward. Difficult circumstances, harmful influences, and broken systems shape the choices people make.

There is truth in both perspectives. Yet Scripture repeatedly introduces a third possibility. It speaks as though human beings live within a larger drama, one that includes influences we cannot always see.

Again and again, the biblical story returns to this theme. A serpent tempts in a garden. A deceiving spirit persuades a king toward destruction. An accuser stands against the righteous. Evil spirits oppress and torment. Satan tempts, whispers, deceives, and seeks opportunities to gain influence. The writers of Scripture do not present evil merely as an abstract idea. They describe it as an active force that works against human flourishing.

Whether one understands these passages literally, symbolically, or some combination of the two, the underlying insight remains powerful. Human beings often experience impulses that seem larger than themselves. We know what is right, yet feel pulled toward what is harmful. We make progress only to find ourselves repeating old patterns. We witness movements of fear, hatred, greed, and violence that spread through individuals and entire communities alike. It can feel at times as though something more than simple ignorance is at work.

Notice, however, that these opposing powers are never portrayed as equal rivals to God. They are adversaries, but they are not ultimate. Their influence is real, yet limited. They tempt, persuade, and deceive, but they do not possess the final word. Even the stories that describe their activity present them as existing within boundaries they did not create and cannot escape.

This perspective invites humility. Not every struggle originates solely within us, nor can every conflict be reduced to external circumstances. The human soul exists within a network of influences—some life-giving, others destructive. Wisdom requires learning to recognize the difference. We are shaped not only by what we choose, but also by what we allow to shape us.

The call, then, is not to live in fear of hidden enemies. It is to remain awake. A person who knows that life includes unseen influences becomes more attentive to the movements of the heart. Not every thought deserves trust. Not every desire deserves obedience. Discernment becomes a form of spiritual vigilance, helping us recognize what leads toward life and what quietly pulls us away from it.
