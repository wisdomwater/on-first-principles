## 2 — The Gift of Unanswered Questions

> *"Some things were delivered plainly, while concerning others the reason was left to be investigated."*

Not every question has an immediate answer.

This can feel disappointing. We often want certainty. We want every mystery explained, every puzzle solved, every uncertainty removed. Yet much of life's deepest wisdom refuses to be rushed.

Some truths are clear enough for anyone to grasp. Love is better than hatred. Mercy heals what vengeance cannot. We are responsible for the choices we make. These are foundations upon which a life can be built. But beyond such foundations lie questions that have occupied thoughtful people for centuries.

What is the soul? What becomes of us after death? Why does freedom exist? How does God work within the world? Such questions do not surrender their answers easily.

Rather than viewing this as a problem, consider it an invitation. A child can learn that music exists. A musician spends a lifetime discovering its depths. The reality does not change, but the capacity to appreciate it grows. Spiritual understanding works much the same way.

There is a difference between information and wisdom. Information can be transferred in an instant. Wisdom develops through reflection, experience, and patient attention. The most important truths are often the ones that unfold gradually.

Questions therefore need not be enemies of faith. Sometimes they are its companions. A question honestly held can become a doorway into greater understanding. It can keep the mind awake and the heart engaged.

This does not mean every answer is equally true. It simply means that some realities are large enough to require a lifetime of exploration. Maturity is not measured by having solved every mystery. It is measured by learning how to live faithfully in the presence of mystery.

The unanswered questions are not always obstacles on the path. Sometimes they are part of the path itself.

*What question has remained with you for years, and what has that question taught you about yourself?*
