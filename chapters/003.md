## 3 — Looking Beyond the Surface

> *"God is light, and in him is no darkness at all."*

Words are powerful, but they have limits.

When we speak about ordinary things, language usually serves us well. We can describe a tree, a river, or a house with reasonable accuracy. But when we begin speaking about God, language starts to strain under the weight of what it is trying to express.

Scripture calls God light. It calls God spirit. It calls God a consuming fire. These images are not meant to reduce God to something familiar. They are meant to point beyond what is familiar toward a reality greater than words can contain.

Consider light. When light enters a dark room, hidden things become visible. Shapes emerge. Colors appear. What was once obscure becomes clear. In the same way, the presence of God illuminates understanding. We begin to see ourselves more honestly. We recognize truth more clearly. We notice realities that were previously hidden from us.

The image of fire works similarly. Fire can destroy, but it can also refine. Metal is purified in a furnace. Impurities are burned away. The divine fire is not concerned with consuming wood or stone. It burns away what keeps us from becoming whole. Pride, bitterness, fear, and falsehood cannot remain unchanged in the presence of truth.

The challenge is that we often stop at the image itself. We see the symbol but miss the reality it points toward. The purpose of spiritual reading is not merely to collect words. It is to learn to see through them.

Many things in life work this way. A wedding ring is more than metal. A flag is more than cloth. A melody is more than vibrating air. The visible form points beyond itself to a deeper meaning. Scripture invites the same kind of seeing.

Wisdom begins when we learn to look beyond the surface and ask what reality is trying to reveal itself through the words.
