## 95 — A Book for Every Stage of the Journey

> *"Just as man consists of body, soul, and spirit, so also does the scripture."*

Not everyone reads Scripture from the same place in life. Some are just beginning their journey. Others have spent years wrestling with difficult questions. Still others have learned to look beyond appearances and search for deeper realities. A book intended for all of them cannot speak in only one way.

This is why sacred writings contain different layers of meaning. The most obvious layer is the literal story itself. A person may read about Abraham, Moses, David, or Jesus and draw encouragement, guidance, and wisdom simply from the events being described. There is nothing wrong with this. In fact, it is good. The surface meaning nourishes those who are taking their first steps, much as simple food nourishes a growing child.

Yet as understanding matures, new dimensions begin to emerge. The stories no longer appear merely as records of what happened long ago. They start to reveal patterns of the inner life. A journey through the wilderness becomes a picture of personal growth. A battle becomes an image of moral struggle. A return from exile becomes a symbol of the soul finding its way back to God. The same text remains before us, but we begin to hear it speaking in a deeper voice.

Beyond this lies a still greater depth. Here the reader is drawn into contemplation of spiritual realities themselves. The stories become transparent, allowing eternal truths to shine through them. The focus shifts from human actions to the larger purposes of God. The text no longer functions merely as instruction or encouragement. It becomes a window through which we glimpse the structure of reality and the movement of divine wisdom throughout creation.

Think of a mountain viewed from a distance. At first you see only its outline. As you draw closer, forests, cliffs, and valleys become visible. Climb higher still, and entire landscapes open before your eyes. The mountain has not changed. What has changed is your capacity to see it. Scripture is much the same. The deeper meanings are not added later; they were present all along, waiting to be discovered.

This understanding also explains why different readers can receive different things from the same passage without contradicting one another. One person may be helped by the practical lesson. Another may find insight into the workings of the soul. A third may perceive a profound spiritual mystery. Each has encountered something real. The text has met each reader at the place where they are able to receive it.

The purpose of Scripture, then, is not merely to convey information. It is to accompany people at every stage of growth. It speaks to beginners without excluding the mature. It offers simple nourishment while also concealing treasures that may take a lifetime to uncover. Like a wise teacher, it adjusts itself to the capacity of the listener, always inviting us to go a little deeper than we have gone before.
